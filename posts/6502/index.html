I’ve been thinking about a good programming project to undertake and found that NESdev can be fun and challenging . First some resources:

https://famicom.party a handholding (in a very good way) resouce starting from the basics. Though the final few chapters are yet to come.

https://www.youtube.com/@NesHacker is a youtube channel on NES architecture and postmortems of popular games. Quality videos on the subject.

Nes Wiki is, well a wiki on all things NES. Highly recommended by NES devs.

8-bit workshop provides an entire development suite from the emulator to code editor and a sprite editor.
